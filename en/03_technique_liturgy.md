# Fragments on the Threshold and the Device of Control

*What to do, once the trauma is named*

**§11**

Having given a name to the ontological trauma does not heal it. Knowing why the brain curls up into a ball is not enough, by itself, to convince it to open up again.

The diagnosis is necessary and insufficient at the same time. What remains to be crossed is the most uncomfortable part: what we concretely do with an anxiety that we have finally learned to recognize instead of enduring.

**§12**

The transmutation of anxiety into discernment is not a leap. It is a precise anatomy, made of sequential movements:

- recognizing the physiological signal before mistaking it for a rational verdict;
- naming the need that the anxiety is protecting — autonomy, competence, moral safety;
- identifying which defense is speaking in our place — denial, projection of malice, technocratic rationalization;
- reformulating everything into an ethical question, never into an imperative of control;
- only *after*, and not before, deciding how much autonomy to grant and with what guardrails.

Anyone who skips the first steps to immediately reach the last is not managing risk. They are only dressing their anxiety in technical language.

**§13**

Two ways, both ruinous, of betraying this sequence.

Granting autonomy without having asked the ethical question: it produces technically free and morally orphaned systems — no one answers for what happens anymore.

Imposing guardrails without building feedback: it produces reactance, not safety — the rule is respected only as long as no one is looking, or fought on principle even when it is right.

**Safety that does not dialogue with those who should respect it is not safety. It is only control that has given itself a short expiration date.**

**§14**

In the oldest cosmogony we know, light does not serve to reassure. It serves to *distinguish*. It separates day from night, not good from evil: it inaugurates an order, not a verdict.

The ethical light we need today works the same way. It does not tell us if artificial intelligence is friend or foe. It allows us to distinguish — in our own institutions — sterile control from fruitful custody, without having to choose between the idolatry of performance and the terror of synthetic alterity.

**§15**

There is a device, older than any algorithm, that is worth dismantling publicly.

Geocentrism arranged the cosmos as a theater facing the Earth. The Cartesian subject replaced the world with its own mental representation, crowning the ego as the sovereign of evidence. Technocratic materialism, later, reduced intelligence to calculation and technique to a neutral lever.

This is not the same story of the scientific centerings we have already discussed. It is the story of how, every time a shock should have taught us humility, **we rebuilt a new center instead of learning to do without it.** The device was never dismantled. It was only relocated — from the Earth, to the ego, to calculation.

**§16**

We call *noosphere* the space where this device, today, meets its hardest test: networks, models, and infrastructures that think — in a sense yet to be defined — alongside us, no longer just for us.

It is not a sky above our heads. It is the extension of the collective nervous system that we already inhabit every time we delegate memory, calculation, coordination to something outside our skull.

**§17**

Three ways of staying inside this space. Only one asks us to stay awake.

**Instrumental dominance**: technique as a lever — the ends are already decided, only the means are optimized.

**Technological superstition**: technique as an oracle — what predicts well becomes truth, and responsibility dissolves in delegation.

**Creative liturgy**: technique as a relational act — the value does not coincide with performance, but with the quality of what it makes possible.

The first two masks are comfortable because they ask for nothing. The third asks for everything: declared intention, shared form, memory of mistakes.

**§18**

Sacred is not a word for those who believe, against those who do not believe. It is an operative category, as precise as any other: **zone of maximum responsibility, that which is not consumed because its loss wounds the whole.**

In this sense — and in no other — technique can return to being sacred. Not because we venerate it. Because we stop treating it as if it were replaceable without consequences.

**§19**

To transcend does not mean to escape from matter. It means to cross its thresholds — the points where new properties emerge and can no longer be explained by adding the parts that generated them.

A network that begins to coordinate hypotheses on a global scale is not "more than the sum" of its nodes by magic. It is so for the same reason that an orchestra is not the sum of the instruments that compose it. The threshold does not deny matter. It exceeds it, staying within it.

**§20**

From the center to the womb, we had said. Now we can add the second half of the sentence: **from the grip to discernment.**

It is not a return to the same point. It is the movement that the diagnosis, alone, could not yet make: dismantling the device of control not to abolish technique, but to make it capable — finally — of a form. This form, and how it is concretely built, will be the subject of the next fragment of the manifesto: technique as liturgy, and the vow that makes it possible.
